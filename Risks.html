Ricks

In our project, we can identify these 3 possible risks that can be calculated. We will not mention unforeseen common risks such as sickness, weddings, or family problems. The following are risks that can be alleviated with proper awareness and planning:

  • Scope creeping: As nothing in life goes perfectly to plan, it is possible that our project will derail from its original scope. This is a concern due to the fact that we plan to include usability testing early in the project, so the scope might change in an attempt to overly please the customer. Or the reason may simply be our lack of experience, or the planning and the original scope might be of low quality.
  • Communication problems: The nature of this project involves many fields, with different people and expertise. A lack of communication in this diverse working environment is a real and serious problem for the project. This can lead to project-ending misunderstandings and mismatched expectations.
  • Technological problems: The creation of a mobile robot that integrates sensors, and navigation functionality while at the same time communicating with a remote device is a complex hardware and software puzzle. With this complexity, there might be some semantic errors or hardware errors that go undetected for a long time.

To combat these risks, our team will include good practices such as diligent version control, frequent stand-up meetings, and thorough testing. However, it is important to establish a healthy mindset in the team regarding risks and uncertainties, as no plans are absolute, and no projects are buttery smooth.
