Scope and Limits

The nature of combining technologies to assemble a new product is that it will take an indefinite amount of time to iterate and improve. Therefore, the development process is considered complete when there is a physical product to publish to a wider audience. This is the only way to realistically move this project beyond the drafting stage, as an interactive product only improves when it interacts with users and receives feedback.

The following are our scope, and deliverables for this project:

  • A basic app with robot connectivity and expected alarm functions.
  • A prototype robot that has 2 navigation modes.
  • Organize a usability test with 5-15 users.
  • Iterate and improve.
  • Release version 1.0 of the product to "beta testers".

The app will have 2 major deliverables. First is the basic alarm functionality and its related supporting features. The second is an interface to visualize and interact with the robot and its functions. As for the robot, the physical creation will be conducted first, the prototype will be considered ready when it’s capable of basic flat surface movement and is equipped with a minicomputer and relevant sensors. For the software of the robot, only 2 functions are expected: a reactive navigation system and a locational navigation system. Lastly, a usability test will be conducted with 5–10 guest users to improve the bare-bones state of the current product.

After a prototype improvement period, the scope of the development project is considered accomplished, as it will now move into the indefinite life cycle of a live-service product, where we continue to develop and improve it based on user feedback and data.
